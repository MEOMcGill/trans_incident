# Canadian Surveys on Attitudes Toward Transgender People

## Canadian Election Study (CES)

### CES 2021 — First explicit transgender question
- **cps21_groupdiscrim_7**: "How much discrimination is there in Canada against... Transgender people?"
  - Response: A great deal / A lot / A moderate amount / A little / None at all
  - Same battery covers: Indigenous peoples, Black people, immigrants, women, men, gays and lesbians, white people
- **cps21_genderid**: Expanded gender demographics (Man / Woman / Non-binary / Other)
- **pes21_groups1_2**: Feeling thermometer for Gays and Lesbians (0–100)
- **pes21_groups1_1**: Feeling thermometer for Feminists (0–100)
- **pes21_feminine_1 / pes21_masculine_1**: Gender identity sliders (0–100)
- **pes21_newerlife**: "Newer lifestyles are contributing to the breakdown of our society" (5-point agree/disagree)
- Conversion therapy attitudes (PES section)
- Data: https://ces-eec.arts.ubc.ca/english-section/surveys/

### CES 2025
- **cps25_groupdiscrim**: Same group discrimination battery fielded again (data not yet publicly available)

### CES 2019
- **cps19_gender**: First wave with trans/non-binary demographic option ("Other: e.g. Trans, non-binary, two-spirit, gender-queer")
- **cps19_groups_therm_4**: Feeling thermometer for Feminists (0–100)
- **pes19_feminine / pes19_masculine**: Gender identity sliders (0–100, split sample)
- **Hostile sexism battery**: pes19_hostile1 ("Most women fail to appreciate all that men do for them"), pes19_hostile2 ("Women seek to gain power by getting control over men"), pes19_hostile3 ("Most women interpret innocent remarks or acts as being sexist")
- **Benevolent sexism battery**: pes19_benevolent1 ("Women should be cherished and protected by men"), pes19_benevolent2 ("Many women have a quality of purity that few men possess"), pes19_benevolent3 ("A good woman ought to be set on a pedestal by her man")
- Data: https://ces-eec.arts.ubc.ca/english-section/surveys/

### CES 2000–2015 (recurring items)
- **Feminism feeling thermometer** (0–100): every wave 2000–2021
- **"Society would be better off if more women stayed home with their children"**: 2000–2015

## Perceived Discrimination / Stigma Surveys

### Abacus Data / Women & Gender Equality Canada — 2SLGBTQI+ Communities Survey (2023)
- **n=3,400**, March 8–20, 2023
- **Q17e (Perceived stigma)**: "How much stigma, if at all, do you think each of the following 2SLGBTQI+ groups face in their daily lives in Canada?" — with "Trans" as one group. Response: A lot / Quite a bit / A little / None / Not sure
- **Q15c (Comfort)**: "How comfortable, or uncomfortable, are you around... People who identify as trans (their gender is not the same as the sex they were assigned at birth)" — Response: Very comfortable / Somewhat comfortable / Somewhat uncomfortable / Very uncomfortable / Not sure
- **Q6 (Broad discrimination)**: "How big a problem, if at all, is discrimination, stigma, and harassment in Canada for each of the following groups?" (2SLGBTQI+ among 12 groups)
- **Result**: 55% perceived that trans individuals face "a lot" or "quite a bit" of stigma
- Report: https://epe.lac-bac.gc.ca/100/200/301/pwgsc-tpsgc/por-ef/women_gender_equality_Canada/2023/111-22-e/POR111-22-Report.html
- **Microdata**: Not publicly released; full report with crosstabs available

### Angus Reid Institute — Transgender Issues (2016, 2023)
- **2016**: n=1,416, September 2016. 85% say trans people face discrimination in daily lives
- **2023**: n=3,016 + 322 non-binary/trans booster, July 2023. 71% say trans people face "heavy discrimination"; 64% say increasing acceptance is a sign of social progress
- Also asked about comfort with trans children, hormone therapy, sports, washroom access, pronouns
- Reports: https://angusreid.org/transgender-issues/ and https://angusreid.org/canada-culture-wars-gender-and-trans-issues/
- **Microdata**: Not publicly available

### Ipsos — LGBT+ Pride Global Survey (annual 2016–2025)
- Canada sample ~1,000 per wave
- 2023: 67% globally agree trans people face "at least a fair amount of discrimination"; 76% agree they should be protected
- 2024: Found declining Canadian support on some measures
- 2018 dedicated survey: Asked about attitudes toward "people who dress and live as one sex even though they were born another"
- Reports: https://www.ipsos.com/en/global-attitudes-toward-transgender-people
- 2024 report: https://www.ipsos.com/sites/default/files/ct/news/documents/2024-05/Pride%20Report%20FINAL_0.pdf
- **Microdata**: Not publicly available

### OHRC "Taking the Pulse" (2017) — Ontario only
- **n=1,501**, early 2017
- **Q8 (Feelings)**: Rate feelings toward "Transgender people" — Very positive / Somewhat positive / Neutral / Somewhat negative / Very negative
- **Q9 (Accommodations)**: Does Ontario do "too much, right amount, or not enough" accommodating transgender people's needs?
- **Q3 (Discrimination reasons)**: "Being transgender" as a listed reason for discrimination
- **Result**: Only 46% of respondents had positive feelings toward trans people
- Report: https://www3.ohrc.on.ca/en/taking-pulse-peoples-opinions-human-rights-ontario/findings
- Questionnaire: https://www.ohrc.on.ca/en/appendix-b-survey-questionnaire-taking-the-pulse

### Williams Institute / Ipsos — 23-Country Survey (2016)
- **n=17,105** across 23 countries including Canada, July–August 2016
- Support for trans rights/anti-discrimination protections; Canada scored above 67 (high support tier)
- Report: https://williamsinstitute.law.ucla.edu/publications/trans-rights-23-country-survey/
- PDF: https://williamsinstitute.law.ucla.edu/wp-content/uploads/Public-Opinion-Trans-23-Countries-Dec-2016.pdf
- **Microdata**: Not publicly available

## Threats, Violence & Discrimination Experiences

### Trans PULSE Canada (2019–2020)
- **n=2,873** trans/NB people, Fall 2019; COVID follow-up n=820, Fall 2020
- Experiences of violence, harassment, healthcare denial among trans/NB people
- Results: https://transpulsecanada.ca/results/
- **Microdata**: By application to research team

### Statistics Canada — Survey of Safety in Public & Private Spaces (SSPPS, 2018, 2024)
- First StatCan survey to collect gender identity data at scale
- 58% of trans/NB people experienced unwanted sexual behaviours in public vs. 23% cisgender
- Data: https://www23.statcan.gc.ca/imdb/p2SV.pl?Function=getSurvey&SDDS=5256
- **Microdata**: Via StatCan Research Data Centres

### Statistics Canada — General Social Survey, Social Identity (Cycle 35, 2020)
- Collected Aug 2020–Feb 2021
- Self-reported discrimination experiences before/during COVID by gender identity
- Gender identity categories: cisgender men/women, transgender men/women, non-binary
- Data: https://www23.statcan.gc.ca/imdb/p2SV.pl?Function=getSurvey&SDDS=5024
- **PUMF**: Available via Borealis Data (https://borealisdata.ca)

### CROP / Fondation Jasmin Roy Sophie Desmarais (2017, 2024)
- **2017 "LGBT Realities"**: n=2,697. Comfort with trans individuals: 27% "very comfortable" having contact; 38% sharing toilets; 31% sharing locker rooms. Only 9% reported having contact with trans people
- **2024 "Sexual and Gender Diversity in Canada"**: n=8,396. 89% of sexually/gender diverse people experienced violence/discrimination over lifetime vs. 69% general population; 46% in past 12 months vs. 23%. Included 1,309 trans and 1,625 non-binary respondents
- Reports: https://fondationjasminroy.com/en/survey-crop/

## Comfort & Acceptance Measures

### Environics / WAGE — Gender Attitudes (2019)
- **n=3,033**, random-probability telephone, May 22–June 27, 2019
- Respondents more comfortable with LGB people than trans/gender non-conforming people
- ~60% believe gender can differ from sex assigned at birth; ~50% agree gender can be other than man/woman
- Report: https://www.canada.ca/en/women-gender-equality/gender-based-violence/research-in-brief/attitudes-regarding-gender-equality-gender-based-violence.html
- Note: This is a separate Environics Research contract, NOT a Focus Canada survey

### Privy Council Office — Comfort Survey (2019)
- Comfort with trans doctor: 79.9% comfortable, 17.6% uncomfortable
- Comfort with trans neighbour: 87.6% comfortable
- Comfort with gay/lesbian/bisexual boss: 90.5% comfortable
- Reported via media: https://globalnews.ca/news/6344287/canadian-government-asked-canadians-comfortable-lgbtq2/
- **Microdata**: Unclear; may be in government public opinion research archives

## Policy Attitudes

### Leger — Transgender Laws (2024)
- **n=2,439** (including Alberta oversample of 1,000), February 9–11, 2024
- Gender reassignment surgeries for minors, puberty blockers/hormones for minors, federal intervention in provincial trans youth policies
- Report: https://leger360.com/transgender-laws/
- PDF: https://leger360.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/04/Leger_-gender-affirming-care_National_Feb-2024-1.pdf
- **Microdata**: Not publicly available
